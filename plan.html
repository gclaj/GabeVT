The Signature Plan

The Green Mountain Compact

Five commitments for Vermont’s next decade. A compact is a promise between people — this one is Gabe’s promise to the voters of the Washington District, written down so you can hold him to it.

1

Build the homes.

Vermont’s housing shortage is the root system of nearly every other crisis. Gabe will push to modernize permitting so good projects move in months, not years; update CHIP so flood-ready designs pencil out; direct growth to places already served by water, sewer, and sidewalks; and rebuild the small-builder ecosystem that once kept Vermont housed.

How you’ll measure it: homes permitted and completed in Washington County, year over year — and how long a permit takes.

2

Fix healthcare costs.

Fully fund a rapid transition to a system where every Vermonter has access to affordable care. Take on administrative waste and drug pricing, protect rural hospitals and clinics from the cost-shift squeeze, and grow the care workforce with housing and training — because access on paper means nothing without a provider in the room.

How you’ll measure it: premium growth, out-of-pocket costs, and whether Washington County keeps — and staffs — its points of care.

3

Win the next generation.

Make Vermont the most competitive place in New England for young workers and families to build careers and businesses. That means confronting the total cost of staying — housing, healthcare, childcare, and the tax burden on year-round residents — and cutting friction for the small employers who create most of our jobs.

How you’ll measure it: net migration of working-age Vermonters, new business starts, and school enrollment trends.

4

Flood-ready by design.

Stop paying for the same flood twice. Invest in mitigation and floodplain restoration up front, make resilient construction financially viable through updated state programs, relocate critical infrastructure where it makes sense, and plan watershed by watershed with towns as full partners.

How you’ll measure it: mitigation dollars deployed before disasters versus recovery dollars after them.

5

Govern with trust.

The Act 181 fight showed what happens when Montpelier legislates at rural Vermont instead of with it. Gabe will hold regular open forums in every corner of the district, put farmers, foresters, and town officials at the table before bills are drafted, and choose common sense over ideology — every issue, every time.

How you’ll measure it: show up to a forum and ask him anything. That’s the point.

Why a compact? Politicians offer promises; builders sign contracts. Gabe has spent his career being held to specifications, budgets, and deadlines. This plan is written the same way — with deliverables you can inspect.
