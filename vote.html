Voter Guide

Make your plan to vote.

Vermont makes voting easy — same-day registration, early voting, and mail ballots. Here’s everything you need for 2026.

Democratic Primary

Tuesday, August 11, 2026

Gabe’s name will appear on the Democratic ballot for State Senate, Washington District. Polls are open until 7:00 p.m.; hours vary by town, so check your town clerk’s posting.

General Election

Tuesday, November 3, 2026

Every active Vermont voter is mailed a general election ballot automatically. Return it by mail, at a drop box, at your town clerk’s office, or vote in person.

Register to vote

Register online in minutes — or register in person at your polling place on Election Day itself. Vermont has same-day registration.

Register at olvr.vermont.gov

Vote early or by mail

Any voter may request an early ballot for the primary — no excuse needed. Track your ballot and manage your registration through your voter portal.

My Voter Page: mvp.vermont.gov

Find your polling place

Polling locations are listed on your My Voter Page and posted by your town clerk. Not sure? Your clerk’s office can answer in one phone call.

Look up your polling place

Who votes in this race?

The Washington District elects three senators to represent Washington County communities — from Barre and Montpelier to Waterbury, Northfield, and the Mad River Valley — in the Vermont State Senate. If you live in the district, you may vote for up to three candidates, and Gabe is asking for one of your three votes.

Questions about your district or ballot? Contact your town clerk or the Vermont Secretary of State’s elections division at sos.vermont.gov/elections.

Frequently asked questions

I’m not registered with a party. Can I vote in the Democratic primary?

Yes. Vermont has open primaries — any registered voter may choose the Democratic ballot at the primary, no party registration required.

I’ll be away on August 11. What do I do?

Request an early/absentee ballot from your town clerk or through mvp.vermont.gov — you can vote by mail or in person at the clerk’s office before Election Day.

I just moved to the district. Can I still vote?

Yes. Register anytime — including on Election Day at your polling place. Bring proof of residence to make it smooth.

Do I need ID to vote in Vermont?

Generally no, if you’re already registered. First-time registrants who registered by mail may need to show identification. When in doubt, bring one — and ask your town clerk.
