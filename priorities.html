Priorities

Where Gabe stands — and what he’ll do.

Vermont’s affordability crisis has three connected drivers: housing supply, healthcare inflation, and the tax burden on year-round residents. You can’t fix one while ignoring the others. Here’s the whole picture.

Sustainable Housing Vermonters Can Afford

Vermont doesn’t have enough homes — and everything from workforce shortages to school budgets to homelessness traces back to that fact. Gabe is the rare candidate who has actually built the housing Vermont says it wants: over $20 million in completed construction, 33 new homes delivered, and 75+ more entitled.

  • Cut the delay tax. Streamline and modernize permitting so a good project doesn’t take years and six figures in carrying costs before the first shovel hits dirt.
  • Update CHIP for flood-ready housing. Let Vermont’s Community Housing and Infrastructure Program reimburse engineered lower levels — elevated podiums and flood-safe parking — so we can build in our village centers and respect the floodplain.
  • Build where the pipes are. Prioritize infill in areas already served by water, sewer, and sidewalks — the most climate-friendly, taxpayer-friendly place to grow.
  • Back the small builders. Most Vermont housing is built by small local firms. Right-size regulation, financing tools, and state programs to how homes actually get built here.

Gabe’s commitment: treat the housing shortage like the emergency it is — with a builder’s urgency, not a task force’s timeline.

Healthcare That Works for Everyone

Healthcare inflation is crushing Vermont families, small businesses, school budgets, and rural hospitals alike. Premiums climb by double digits while access shrinks. Gabe believes Vermont must stop managing the decline and start funding the fix.

  • Fully fund a rapid transition to a healthcare system where every Vermonter has access to affordable care.
  • Attack cost drivers head-on — administrative bloat, prescription drug pricing, and the cost-shifting that punishes rural providers.
  • Protect rural access. A hospital or clinic closure in Washington County isn’t an inconvenience — it’s a life-safety issue and an economic body blow.
  • Grow the healthcare workforce with housing, training pipelines, and licensing reform — because you can’t staff a hospital where no nurse can afford to live.

Gabe’s commitment: “We need to fully fund a rapid transition to a healthcare system where everyone has access to affordable care.”

An Economy That Keeps Our Kids Here

Vermont is aging faster than almost any state, and every year we export talented young people we can’t afford to lose. Gabe’s goal is simple and measurable: make Vermont the most competitive place in New England for young workers and families to build careers and businesses.

  • Lower the cost of staying. Housing and healthcare are the two biggest bills a young family pays — fixing them is economic development.
  • Ease the burden on year-round residents. Review the total tax load working Vermonters carry, and stop treating them as the balance wheel for every budget gap.
  • Champion small business. Cut friction for the trades, startups, farms, and Main Street employers who create most of our jobs.
  • Invest in what attracts. Childcare, broadband, and vibrant downtowns are the modern recruiting pitch for the workforce Vermont needs.

Gabe’s commitment: measure every economic policy against one test — does it make it easier for a young Vermont family to stay and thrive?

Flood-Ready, Resilient Communities

Montpelier, Barre, and the Winooski and Mad River valleys know the cost of flooding firsthand. Vermont’s village centers sit where the water goes — and they’re also where our housing, jobs, and infrastructure already are. Gabe has proposed concrete fixes to build resilience into how we grow.

  • Rebuild smarter, before the next storm. Fund mitigation and floodplain restoration on the front end instead of paying multiples in disaster recovery on the back end.
  • Make resilience buildable. Update state programs — starting with CHIP — so flood-resilient construction like elevated podiums is financially viable, not penalized.
  • Move critical infrastructure to higher ground where it makes sense, and harden what must stay.
  • Plan watershed by watershed, with towns as partners rather than bystanders.

Gabe’s commitment: every dollar of recovery spending should buy a Vermont that floods less next time.

Working Lands & Rural Trust

The fight over Act 181 taught Montpelier a lesson Gabe has been vocal about: you cannot build durable environmental policy without the trust of the people who care for most of the land. Ideological overreach — rules written without understanding how rural communities live, work, and steward their acreage — sets conservation back.

  • Listen before legislating. Land-use policy should be written with farmers, foresters, and rural towns at the table from day one.
  • Correct course on overreach. Fix provisions that treat rural Vermont as an afterthought while keeping the real gains for housing and smart growth.
  • Reward stewardship. Support the working farms and forests that keep land open, productive, and healthy.
  • Choose common sense over ideology — on every issue, every time.

Gabe’s commitment: rural Vermont will have a senator who shows up, listens first, and never mistakes a spreadsheet for a hillside farm.

Welcoming Communities, Growing Workforce

Vermont’s deepest long-term challenge is demographic: we need more workers, students, and neighbors. As a board member of the U.S. Committee for Refugees and Immigrants, Gabe has worked nationally on exactly this — and he’s written forcefully that Vermont’s future depends on restoring legal immigration pathways.

  • Advocate for working legal immigration pathways that bring Vermont the nurses, farmworkers, students, and entrepreneurs our economy needs.
  • Expand state-level support for refugees, international students, and new American workers.
  • Build housing for newcomers — because welcome without a roof is just a word.
  • Partner with employers who rely on global talent, from farms to hospitals to colleges.

Gabe’s commitment: keep Vermont what it has always been at its best — a place that makes room.
