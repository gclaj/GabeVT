Meet Gabe

A life spent showing up.

In uniform. In the classroom. On the planning commission. On the job site. Gabe Lajeunesse has never waited for someone else to solve the problem in front of him.

From service to statehouse

Gabe Lajeunesse served as a U.S. Air Force officer specializing in international affairs. His service took him to the highest levels of national decision-making — advising the Joint Chiefs of Staff and serving on White House interagency committees under Presidents Bush and Obama. He learned early that real leadership means working across differences to get hard things done.

Gabe earned his Juris Doctor at Georgetown University Law Center, and he has taught economic policy, international affairs, and law at the undergraduate and graduate levels — at Georgetown, at Hamline, and at Norwich University right here in Vermont.

A builder in every sense

When Gabe looked at Vermont’s housing crisis, he didn’t write a white paper — he picked up a shovel. Through his small development firm, he has completed more than $20 million in housing construction, delivering 33 new homes for Vermont families and securing entitlements for more than 75 additional units. He knows exactly where Vermont’s housing pipeline gets stuck, because he’s lived every stage of it.

That firsthand knowledge is why his voice carries in the state’s housing debates — from modernizing the Community Housing and Infrastructure Program to build flood-ready homes, to insisting that land-use reform earn the trust of the rural Vermonters who steward most of our land.

Rooted in Washington County

Gabe lives in Montpelier with his wife, Kristen. They raised three children here — including two sons who followed their father into service as graduates of West Point, now commissioned officers in the U.S. Army.

Gabe serves his community as a Montpelier Planning Commissioner and as an appointee to Vermont’s Community Development Board. Nationally, he serves on the board of the U.S. Committee for Refugees and Immigrants, working to protect displaced people and connect newcomers with the communities — like ours — that need them.

Gabe at a glance

The path forward begins with humility, continues with listening, and ends with a commitment to lead from shared unity and common ground.

— Gabe Lajeunesse, VTDigger, April 2026

Why He’s Running

Because Vermont’s problems are solvable.

Gabe is running for State Senate because the three crises squeezing Vermont families — housing, healthcare, and affordability — are connected, and because he has spent his career doing exactly this kind of work: building coalitions, mastering complex systems, and delivering results you can see.
