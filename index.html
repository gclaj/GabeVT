Meet Gabe

Service is the through-line.

Gabe served his country as a U.S. Air Force officer specializing in international affairs — including as an advisor to the Joint Chiefs of Staff and on White House interagency committees under two presidents. He earned his law degree at Georgetown and has taught economic policy, international affairs, and law at Georgetown, Hamline, and Vermont’s own Norwich University.

At home in Montpelier, he’s a husband, a dad, a Planning Commissioner, an appointee to Vermont’s Community Development Board — and a small-scale housing builder who has put real roofs over real Vermonters’ heads.

The Signature Plan

The Green Mountain Compact

Five commitments for Vermont’s next decade — specific enough to be held accountable to, practical enough to actually pass.

1. Build the homes

Unlock housing in every county with faster permitting and smarter incentives.

2. Fix healthcare costs

Fund a rapid transition to affordable care for every Vermonter.

3. Win the next generation

Make Vermont New England’s best place to start a career or a business.

4. Flood-ready by design

Rebuild smarter before the next storm, not after it.

5. Govern with trust

Listen first. Legislate with rural Vermont, not at it.
